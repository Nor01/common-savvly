Savvly vs. Annuities

Annuities Get a Bad Rap for a Good Reason

Annuities are expensive and complex products sold by insurance companies. They are sold as a way to provide a steady income, a type of "protection." But that protection comes at a cost. Buyers pay high fees for annuities, and they will lose out on returns that could have been better. An annuity’s fixed or variable return may be much lower than the return of a diversified portfolio of stocks and bonds. And, because annuity payouts are low and unchanging, they are unlikely to protect consumers against inflation.

After the financial crisis of 2008, annuities became even less valuable. Since then, the insurance industry has changed to protect itself by providing limited benefits to clients. Because of this, many financial advisors continue to advise against purchasing annuities.

Savvly’s Secret Sauce

Savvly changes the rules related to how consumers pool their savings. The pooling mechanism allows Savvly users to support each other in a shared network.
